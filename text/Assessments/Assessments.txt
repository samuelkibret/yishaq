There have been several changes to buildings in Massawa due to several circumstances. One of the significant incidents that changed the urban fabric of the port was the Independence War. During this time, most of the southeast part of the city was severely damaged, changing the entire urban fabric. 
Some of the buildings in the city, such as Banca d’Italia, have been severely deteriorated during the independence war and haven’t been maintained appropriately. In contrast, some parts of the city have been kept intact and in fairly good condition. There have also been some additions to the site. Most of the new additions to the site outside of the ring road were demolished, maintaining the original urban fabric of the city.
